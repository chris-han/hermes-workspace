## Purpose

The About page should answer one question:

```text
Why are we called Semantier?
```

The answer:

```text
Because AI-native organizations need the Semantic Tier: the governed enterprise tier where meaning, authority, responsibility, and action become explicit before AI-generated work can enter mission-critical execution. This tier is the foundation of Certainty Infrastructure.
```

The page should deepen the landing page thesis:

```text
Don’t align the mind.
Govern the action.
```

The About page explains that action governance is only possible when the organization has a place where meaning is governed. That place is the Semantic Tier.

Use `SEMANTIER_VISION_STORYLINE_HERO_QUEST.md` as the emotional and strategic storyline for this page: the future AI-native organization problem, the risk of organizational Brownian motion, the rejection of AI as a new responsible subject, and the mission to build the Semantic Tier.

## Core Explanation

Semantier combines two ideas:

```text
Semantic: meaning, interpretation, concepts, rules, and organizational understanding.
Tier: an architectural tier, not a feature, not a chatbot, and not a dashboard.
```

Semantier means:

```text
The semantic tier for AI-native organizations.
```

This is the core governance place where an organization defines:

```text
What facts are accepted
What those facts mean
Who has authority to interpret them
Which policies apply
Which actions are allowed
Which exceptions must escalate
How decisions can be replayed later
```

## Page Argument

The About page should make one argument clearly:

```text
AI-native organizations cannot be governed only at the model tier or workflow tier.
They need a semantic tier between human value anchors and agentic action.
```

Why:

```text
Models generate.
Workflows route.
Databases store.
Dashboards display.

But none of these alone decides what an action means, who has authority, whether it is legitimate, and whether it can be trusted later.
```

Semantier exists to fill that missing tier.

### 2. Why The Name

Section title:

```text
Why “Semantier”?
```

Body:

```text
Every organization already has semantics: the meanings behind customer, employee, revenue, risk, approval, exception, role, and responsibility. In traditional organizations, those meanings live in people’s heads, policy documents, spreadsheets, meetings, and informal judgment.

AI-native organizations cannot rely on implicit meaning. Once AI experts start proposing actions across finance, HR, compliance, sales, delivery, procurement, and operations, meanings must become explicit, governed, versioned, and replayable.

Semantier is named for this missing tier: the semantic tier.
```

Short version:

```text
Semantier = Semantic + Tier.
```

### 3. What Is The Semantic Tier?

Section title:

```text
The Semantic Tier is where meaning becomes governance.
```

Body:

```text
The Semantic Tier is the organizational tier that sits between human value anchors and AI-generated action. It translates purpose, policy, authority, and responsibility into machine-checkable governance structures.
```

It governs:

```text
Facts: what the organization accepts as real.
Concepts: what business terms mean in context.
Authority: who or what has the right to decide.
Policy: which rules apply and in what order.
Justification: why an action is proposed.
Admissibility: whether the action is allowed.
Replay: whether the decision can be reconstructed later.
```

One-line summary:

```text
The Semantic Tier is where human value becomes executable governance.
```

### 4. Why AI-Native Organizations Need It

Section title:

```text
AI makes implicit meaning dangerous.
```

Body:

```text
In a human-only organization, semantic drift is slow. People misunderstand terms, interpret policies differently, and escalate exceptions through meetings.

In an AI-native organization, semantic drift can happen at machine speed. Different AI experts may interpret the same customer, employee, invoice, approval, risk, or policy in different ways. Each interpretation may look plausible locally, while the organization loses coherence globally.
```

Key point:

```text
The more AI experts an organization deploys, the more it needs a shared semantic tier.
```

Three risks without it:

```text
Plausible but unauthorized actions
Fast but inconsistent decisions
Automated work that cannot be explained, replayed, or audited
```

The governed semantic tier that connects human value anchors to AI expert action.

### 7. Finance And HR As First Semantic Domains

Section title:

```text
The first semantic domains: money and people.
```

Body:

```text
Semantier begins with Finance and HR because they govern the two foundational realities of every organization: money and people.
```

Finance:

```text
Finance governs economic facts: cash, revenue, cost, margin, tax, budget, risk, obligations, and external trust.
```

HR:

```text
HR governs organizational order and trust: people, roles, authority, responsibility, compensation, decision quality, and learning.
```

Why this matters:

```text
An AI-native organization cannot safely execute financial actions without knowing who has authority. It cannot safely execute people decisions without knowing which policies, roles, responsibilities, and trust conditions apply. Finance and HR are separate functions, but they meet inside the Semantic Tier.

Semantier's HR thesis is not that reliable people are found once through screening. That is a static theory of people. People change; context changes; incentives change; decision quality changes. Organizational execution comes from trust, and trust has to be governed.

That governance should not mean real-time surveillance of every employee action. It should mean "trust, but verify": support trusted decisions and evaluate decision quality through continuous decision trajectories, including evidence, authority, policy fit, exceptions, corrections, and replayable outcomes.
```
